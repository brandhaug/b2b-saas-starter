Stripe is the provider behind the billing Optional Provider Module. The integration is scaffolded as an Optional Provider Module: pricing content renders without Stripe, and `moduleConfigStatus('billing', …)` in `packages/env/src/server.ts` reports `env-present` once `STRIPE_SECRET_KEY` and `STRIPE_WEBHOOK_SECRET` are set. Checkout, portal redirect, and webhook handler routes are planned but not yet implemented in `apps/{web,api,background}/src`.

## Env gate

Stripe env recognition requires `STRIPE_SECRET_KEY` and `STRIPE_WEBHOOK_SECRET` (see `moduleConfigStatus` in `packages/env/src/server.ts` and the secret matrix in `ARCHITECTURE.md`). Missing either keeps the integration inactive — public pricing still renders, and subscribe buttons fall back to a "Configure billing" message. Runtime activation waits for checkout, portal, webhook, and plan-state routes.

## Webhook handling

Inbound Stripe webhooks will land on the background Worker (the consumer listed in the secret matrix). The route, signature verification, plan-state updates, and `auditEvents` writes are not yet implemented — the env gate exists so the billing Optional Provider Module reports `needs-config` until configured.

## Customer portal

A workspace customer-portal redirect is part of the planned billing surface. The route is not yet implemented in `apps/web` or `apps/api`, and ARCHITECTURE.md notes that workspace-role enforcement is not wired at the route layer yet — both land together when billing activates.

## What Stripe is not used for

Stripe is not used for user authentication, not for invitation emails, and not for storing PII beyond the customer ID and last-four card display. The starter's domain stays in D1.
